About Me

Right now I'm finishing off my PhD in Augmented Reality at Durham University exploring some pretty interesting Computer Vision problems and software design challenges. When I'm not working on that I'm usually pushing pixels around the web, smudging pencil sketches, or worrying about gaining an extra millisecond of efficiency out of some code. When I do manage to break free from my computer, I get out cycling or disappear into my kitchen and brew up some craft beer.

I started freelancing in 2010, working on projects in identity design, website design, application interface design, and software engineering. I've gained a lot of experience in industry standards and design theory throughout that time. In addition to web development, I have recently started branching out into mobile development, both for the web and with native applications.

Technical Skills

Whether designing or developing I'm a strong believer in agile methodologies to rapidly prototype. This means getting straight into the code, and getting something working quickly. It provides working sites or applications to the client much faster rather than relying on image based mockups for the earliest design phase.

With my background in Software Engineering, I can develop custom back-ends for websites, but I'm also experienced in modifying existing content management systems, such as Wordpress and Perch, to meet the clients needs.

I think typography can make or break a design, and pay close attention to the mix of fonts in use. All of my designs are based start with the typography, which in turn influences the spacing of other elements.

This site was handcrafted in the browser to the latest HTML5/CSS3 standards. The headings are set in Brandon Grotesque and the body is Prenton, courtesy of Typekit.
